02. Clear Space & Minimum Size
Usage Guidelines
These rules ensure the mark remains legible and uncluttered across all applications — from a worship guide cover to a 16px favicon.

Minimum Clear Space

Maintain a clear zone around the full mark equal to the height of the cross arm (distance from crossbar top to leaf base, marked as "x"). This ensures legibility in all layouts. Apply to both stacked and horizontal lockups.

Minimum Size

At 32px width, the icon retains the cross, leaf, and three main roots. At 20px (favicon), simplify to cross + leaf silhouette + two outer roots only. For print, minimum width is 12mm. Below these thresholds, drop the secondary leaf, tendrils, and veins.

03. Design Notes
Construction & Intent
The decisions behind the mark — what each element means, how it scales, and where it lives best.

Theology

The cross is both the instrument of death and the source of new life. The roots represent the deep, hidden work of discipleship — the parts no one sees. The leaf is the visible fruit. Three main roots echo the Trinity. The second unfurling leaf implies ongoing transformation, not a finished state.

Colour Story

This is the only mark that naturally uses all three palette anchors within the icon itself: charcoal for structure, sage for life, and clay for earthiness. The roots in clay literally ground the mark in Risen's visual language, while the sage leaf introduces growth without breaking the palette.

Scalability

Built in layers of detail that peel away naturally at small sizes. Full detail (veins, tendrils, second leaf) at 64px+. Simplified (cross, one leaf, three root strokes) at 32px. Silhouette (cross + leaf + two roots) at favicon. No manual redrawing needed — just let the fine strokes fall below rendering threshold.

Applications

The organic shapes lend themselves to textured printing — letterpress, deboss on kraft paper, foil on worship guides. The sage monotone works as a sub-brand for discipleship materials (small groups, mentoring, Bible study). The charcoal monotone is ideal for stamps, watermarks, and single-colour printing.
